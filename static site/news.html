18th December 2014

It was the boys christmas disco at school this afternoon and Jacob has been working on a very special goal for the event.

He is part of a new program in Oxfordshire called the Move Program which really focuses on bringing together goals set by the child and family to make everyday life a bit easier or achieving something the child really wants to do. It's a lovely idea to get the children motivated and to bring all the professionals together and to make sure they are listening to what the child wants to achieve not just doing Physio for the sake of it. So anyway, Jacob's goal was to be a DJ! Nothing too tricky then Jacob!

The school, Physio's and jacobs helpers all being so fabulous said they would arrange it for the end of term disco and in order to be able to achieve this he had to stand by himself only holding on with one hand (in order to be able to change songs with the other! ) to begin with he could only stand holding two hands for about 10 secs, after lots of work he got to one handed holding but very short sessions and by today he was able to hold the table with just one hand for 2 whole songs!!! It was an amazing moment, he had all his "fans" screaming for him and singing the words back to him! Jordan was leading the dancing with all the boys!

28th October 2013

4 months post op! So we are now into half term. Can't believe how quickly the time has gone. The boys have been doing brilliantly at school and are really enjoying being there. After being pretty knocked out by a cold for the last few days, Jacob has been a bit tired and not been up to doing too much. So last night I told him that if he can crawl across the rug then I will buy him a new cd, hoping this might motivate him, (He hasn't done any independent crawling since the op, and even before the op it was very minimal and a big struggle) he got upset last night and said he can't do it and it's hard. I felt pretty bad for trying to get him to do it as I know crawling is one of the most difficult things for him, so I just promised to help him and reassured him that it would take time but with practice he will get there.

Anyway, he must have thought about it because this morning he woke up and said "I'm going to crawl now Mummy I want my cd" and sure enough he crawled right across the rug! He was so pleased. He then had physio first thing and I was unsure whether to take his wheelchair or see how far we could get walking. Jordan promised to help if Jacob got tired so we left the chair in the car. He walked all the way from the car park, into the hospital, up in the lift and into community paediatrics. By far the longest walk he's ever done in one go! Followed by a fabulous physio session! We then stopped in the cafe and a lovely couple said how well behaved they were, gave them some pocket money each and bought us all a bar of chocolate! Jacob still had energy and so walked all the way back to the car! 

We then of course went straight into town to get his promised cd, (He chose pop party 12) and Jordan chose a toy bus for being a fab boy all day. Whilst Nanny and Great Grandma were visiting Jacob wanted to show off his new crawling skills and even managed to get into the crawling position himself! Something never done before! 

All in all a fantastic day just time for swimming therapy now before bed.

Wednesday 17th July 2013

So, Jacob is still working extremely hard at his Physio and stretches every day. He is using his walker with just me me holding his t-shirt now (for confidence) I cant wait to post a video of him walking in his walker totally on his own soon! 

We had an appointment with ophthalmology yesterday to check out his eyes and squint. Fabulous news is that he doesn't need glasses, his vision is actually good and they are not making any difference! (No more replacing glasses every week or so!)

He does however need surgery to correct his squint in both eyes. His muscles are too tight and are pulling his eyes in and up, which is resulting in him seeing double a lot of the time, particularly when he looks down. This means his fine motor skills are being really effected, as it very difficult for him to see what he is doing and it is also affecting his sitting and posture all things which are very difficult for him anyway! I wish we had known how much it is affecting him sooner, but anyway it looks like surgery will hopefully be scheduled for Thursday. It's worrying that he needs to be put to sleep again but compared to SDR it is fairly minor surgery. Should take about 45 mins and he should be out the same day. Will let you know exactly what day and time when we do, but hopefully it will make a huge difference to him again. 

On another note, another massive achievement for Jacob, he was swimming around the pool in a rubber ring totally by himself! He could support himself up and his little legs were going so fast! Will try and post a video tomorrow. Both boys were very excited to be able to swim about by themselves and we are very proud of them.

Monday 1st July 2013 - Day 4 Post Operation

It was a much better day today. Jacob did really well in both physio sessions and is eating for England again. Pizza and fries are on their way and he can't wait!

He did a lot of rolling today and some crawling practice. It is really hard work for him at the moment but he's trying very hard and the physio said after he had been doing it for a little while he needed less support. Same with sit to stand, he got stronger after doing a couple of reps which is great. 

He is brilliant at his stretches and seems to be using his legs separately for the first time. You can see his brain ticking away and making new connections.

He did a couple of exercises that he couldn't do before too which is amazing (lying on his tummy and lifting legs one at a time to kick his bottom) seems simple but he couldn't do them before as his brain was just sending mixed messages to his legs so it's great to see this has improved! 

He even got to ride a trike around the therapy centre today but his steering needs a little more practice.

Had a bit of an 'oh my god I can't believe he has actually had SDR' moment today. Reality kind of caught up with me but it feels good.

Thanks everyone for your continued support. Night all xxx

Back up to the room and he had some pediasure but couldn't keep it down again and now he's sleeping. 

We're allowed to take him for a ride in his wheelchair about 6pm so I'm going to show him the beautiful rooftop garden which should cheer him up! 

Keep your fingers crossed for the sickness to stop and for tomorrow to be even better.

Thanks for all your support xxx

30th November 2012 - David Cameron Meeting

We had a very important meeting tonight with David Cameron to discuss SDR.
